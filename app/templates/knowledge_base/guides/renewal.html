Permit Renewal Guide

Guide to renewing your organization's permit in Uganda

Permit Renewal Process Overview

Note: This guide provides general information about the permit renewal process. For personalized guidance, use the Permit Renewal Module in NGOmply.

When to Renew

Organizations should begin the renewal process at least 3 months before the expiry of their current permit. Permits are typically valid for:

  • NGOs: 5 years
  • CBOs: 1-2 years (varies by district)

Step 1: Prepare Required Documents

Gather the following documents for renewal:

  • Completed Form H (Application for Renewal of Permit)
  • Copy of current permit/certificate
  • Annual reports for the period since last renewal
  • Audited financial statements for the period since last renewal
  • Updated workplan and budget
  • Tax clearance certificate from URA
  • Proof of payment of renewal fees

Step 2: Pay Renewal Fees

Renewal fees vary depending on the type of organization:

  • Local NGO: UGX 80,000
  • Foreign NGO: USD 100
  • International NGO: USD 300
  • Regional NGO: USD 200
  • CBO: Varies by district (typically UGX 30,000 - 50,000)

Step 3: Submit Application

Submit your application to the appropriate authority:

  • NGOs: Submit to the NGO Bureau
  • CBOs: Submit to the District Community Development Office

Step 4: Follow Up

After submission:

  • Keep track of your application status
  • Respond promptly to any requests for additional information
  • Attend any interviews or meetings if required

Step 5: Collect Renewed Permit

Once approved, collect your renewed permit from the issuing authority.

Common Reasons for Renewal Rejection

  • Incomplete documentation
  • Non-compliance with reporting requirements
  • Tax non-compliance
  • Operating outside approved objectives or geographical area
  • Governance issues or complaints against the organization
