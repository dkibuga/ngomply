CBO Registration Guide

Complete guide to registering a Community Based Organization in Uganda

Registration Process Overview

Note: This guide provides general information about the CBO registration process in Uganda. For personalized guidance, use the Registration Module in NGOmply.

Step 1: Prepare Required Documents

Before registering your CBO, you need to prepare the following documents:

  1. Draft Constitution
  2. Complete CBO Form K (Application for Registration)
  3. Prepare Annual Work Plan and Budget
  4. Prepare Governance Structure Chart
  5. Prepare Statement on Funding Sources

Step 2: Submit to DNMC

Submit your application to the District Non-Governmental Monitoring Committee (DNMC):

  1. Pay prescribed fees
  2. Submit all required documents to the District Community Development Officer
  3. Attend an interview with the DNMC if required

Step 3: Obtain Registration Certificate

After approval, collect your CBO Registration Certificate from the District Community Development Office.

Required Documents

  • CBO Constitution
  • List of Founding Members with their signatures
  • Minutes of the meeting that elected the Executive Committee
  • Workplan and Budget
  • Organizational Structure
  • Proof of Payment of Registration Fees
  • Recommendation letters from Local Council (LC1 and LC3)

Fees

Registration fees for CBOs vary by district but typically range from UGX 50,000 to UGX 100,000.

Important Considerations

  • CBOs are registered at the district level and operate within that district only
  • If you wish to operate in multiple districts, consider registering as an NGO instead
  • CBOs must submit annual reports to the District Community Development Office
  • Registration must be renewed periodically (typically every 1-2 years)
